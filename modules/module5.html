Connecting the Healthcare Ecosystem at a National Level

5.1 NPHIES: Saudi Arabia's Backbone

The National Platform for Health Insurance Exchange Services (NPHIES) is the cornerstone of Saudi Arabia's digital health transformation. It connects healthcare providers and insurance companies for seamless approval and claims processing.

Provider Services

  • Eligibility Check
  • Pre-Authorization
  • Claims Submission

Payer Services

  • Automated Adjudication
  • Payment Reconciliation
  • Policy Management

5.2 Global Context: TEFCA (USA)

While NPHIES focuses on insurance and claims in KSA, the USA's TEFCA (Trusted Exchange Framework and Common Agreement) creates a 'network of networks' for clinical data exchange nationwide.

Why This Matters for Saudi Arabia?

Understanding TEFCA principles helps in designing robust clinical data exchanges like 'Seha' (Saudi's Unified Health App) and future HIE (Health Information Exchange) initiatives in the region.

5.3 Seha Virtual Hospital: A Case Study

Saudi Arabia launched the world's largest Virtual Hospital, connecting over 150 hospitals across the Kingdom. It provides specialized remote care using advanced telemedicine and AI technologies.

  • Supports 30+ specialized core specialties
  • Reduces need for patient travel to major cities
  • Uses IoT for remote patient monitoring
